Context
One system for five product areas. No tooling existed.

Remote helps companies hire and pay global teams across 180+ countries. Enterprise HR operators needed to run bulk salary cycles, contract amendments, and location changes — but were doing it manually, one employee at a time, using spreadsheets.

I was Design Lead for a shared bulk-upload framework covering five distinct product areas: Global Payroll, Employee Lifecycle, HRIS, EOR onboarding, and Contractor Invoices.

Problems
Four blockers preventing a core enterprise workflow.
01

No path for scaleOne-by-one manual edits were the only option. For teams managing hundreds of employees across countries, it was a hard blocker.

02

Missing featureCompetitors supported bulk updates. It was becoming a reason not to renew.

03

Hidden compliance rulesCountry-specific constraints — wage floors, consent laws, works councils — weren't visible at point of action. Users discovered them by failing.

04

Fear of errorA wrong bulk edit can affect payroll across dozens of employees in multiple jurisdictions. Operators avoided the action entirely.

Goal
One north star.

Create a safe, efficient, scalable way to manage bulk uploads.

Research
I talked to HR teams and the people who cleaned up after them.

I interviewed HR operators and Legal and Payroll — the teams who owned the downstream consequences of every bulk action. That perspective changed the entire problem definition.

01

85% of users ran mass salary or title updates at least bi-annually. This was a core workflow with no product support.

02

Most were already using spreadsheets as a workaround — they weren't asking for something new, just for the product to catch up.

03

Fear of compliance errors — not usability — was the primary reason operators avoided bulk changes. The problem was trust.

Design process
Five iterations. Each one answered a specific question.

I shipped the simplest version that could produce real user data, measured where it failed, and iterated from there.

01 CSV upload 40% drop-off
02 CSV + real-time validations Drop-off → 12%
03 Inline table editor 90% user preference
04 Before/after diff preview Confident decisions
05 Undo window + audit log Full trust loop closed
Final design
One framework. Five use cases. Zero guesswork at point of action.

The final design combines multi-format input (CSV, Excel, or manual) with built-in compliance guardrails, a real-time inline editor, and a complete audit trail.

↳
